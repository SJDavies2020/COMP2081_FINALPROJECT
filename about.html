About Lazy Tulip Cafe

The Lazy Tulip Cafe was opened to create a mindful environment for persons to visit while enjoying a healthy, nutritious meal. When you walk into the cafe you get this Bohemian vibe, the enivironment is open and welcoming, a nice place for a coffee with a friend.

When Michelle envisioned creating a cafe, she wanted to create a home away from home environment. Michelle brought her expertise in tourism and marketing to give birth to the Lazy Tulip Cafe. Michelle's main focus other than the enivronment was to create meals that were healthy and that catered to persons from all walks of life and with food sensitivities. The gluten-free and vegetarian options allow the cafe to expand the target market. The Lazy Tulip Cafe is one of few cafes in the Barrie area where you could come for coffee and stay for lunch. That is what sets it apart from the other coffee shops. You can have more than cofee and a baked good. There are soups, smoothies, salads, sandwiches and more.

What is more inspiring is the cafes link to the Barrie and Ontario community. Most of the ingredients used to make the meals and drinks in the cafe are sourced from within Ontario. The coffee, comes from Guelph, the tea is sourced from Toronto, and even the eggs come from a local farm within Ontario. The cafe believes in a sense of community, that's why it works so hard to support local business owners and farmers, as its way of giving back to the community, but the cafe also supports local artists. From time to time, the cafe highlights the work of local artists through art or photography by featuring a small article showcasing their work and talent.

The diversity of the staff at Lazy Tulip Cafe adds to the mystique of the cafe, the personalities, and the experiences, adds to the quality of the service and the entire experience at Lazy Tulip Cafe.
